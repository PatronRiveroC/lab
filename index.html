About Me

Carlos Patron-Rivero

I work on the evolution of morphometric traits across different ecological and geographical contexts. My research focuses on macroevolution, ecomorphology, and the evolution of color patterns in reptiles.

One of my main interests is understanding the biological and ecological information we can derive from natural history collections and fieldwork. I integrate ecological niche modeling, morphometric analyses, and phenotypic data to investigate the drivers of diversification and phenotypic convergence.

Research

I am broadly interested in how morphology evolves in response to environmental gradients. My work combines ecological niche modeling, geometric morphometrics, and color pattern analysis to explore macroevolutionary processes, particularly in reptiles.
